The Last Statements of Texas Death Row Inmates

About the data

Below are different methods of presenting data of executed offenders under the Texas Department of Criminal Justice. The earliest recorded execution from this dataset was on December 7, 1982 and the latest was on October 14, 2015. There are 530 executions total. The ages of offenders executed range from 24 to 67 years of age. The racial categories of those executed are: Black, White, Hispanic, and Other(one offender). As a group we took the data provided and presented it in various ways. Scroll down to see how the presentation of data changes how we understand it!

On our data choices

Since the data set we were visualizing was centred around verbal statements, it made the most sense for us to use word clouds as the focus of our project. We have separated the data into sections based on race and created clouds for each separate group. To complement these clouds, we are also providing demographic information from the dataset in more varied ways, focusing on the relationship between age and race to give viewers an idea of the different people who have ended up on death row in Texas. Many of the inmates chose not to give a final statement. Their data has still been included in the demographic information.
